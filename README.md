# US-East cheaters: Now in a handy database!

In the absence of a truly robust and accurate† cheater list for US-East, we've decided to put together a list of cheaters on Sterling, VA servers. Although most of them are stupid enough to get a server ban, we've found plenty with otherwise clean slates. It's well past time to change that. Our goal is to take note of every cheater against whom we play; it became apparent long ago that the real strength of databases such as this is the ability to track human cheaters, not bots. The latter are extremely obvious.

The vast majority of markings will have some sort of incontrovertible evidence backing them up. This might be something as straightforward as a video in which they do something only a cheater could do to an archived screenshot where both teams' classes are visible, as in one case. An entry will never *lack* an explanation under `proof`, but if it's an obvious rage hacker whose friends list and comment sections are full of cheaters, it could simply say "aimbotting + server bans"; we aim to meet the standard of "evident beyond a reasonable doubt" and consider community server bans by automated anti-cheats to fulfil this, so people with such bans may or may not have accompanying video evidence. We do not mark people as suspicious: either there is evidence that someone is cheating, or there isn't. The evidence for each mark is available directly within the [list](https://github.com/anaemicelle/tf2bd/blob/main/playerlist.sterling.json). 

Every marking is accompanied by 100% certainty on our part that the player is cheating, but it's just not possible to, say, get a clip of every single person doing something completely impossible and run `status` in console. (It sounds easy before you get into a game; we can typically detect particularly obvious cheaters within seconds just by the killfeed, but then marking them "because I know he was cheating" just won't fly.) This is why robust explanations are provided for the more questionable markings, which are usually the people *not* rage hacking and who have no server bans; the idea is that if someone is doing that, he's probably not going to be hiding it in your game, either. I may later add to this repo my personal list, which provides no evidence but follows the same criterion that I must be 100% certain that player is cheating for him to be marked. But you should never trust that someone is cheating just because someone says he is; combine these resources with your own intuition and experience as a player and you will seldom be wrong.

† The Uncletopia and "TF2BD Trusted" lists, which are both run by the same person and group, are robust, but not completely accurate; I found many false positives with minimal searching. They range from understandably jumping the gun to [pants-on-head insane](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SZUbLdi7pfk). But they're generally good, that is, if you think someone's cheating, check his history, and he's marked on one of those lists, he probably is. It's useful enough regardless that part of the exigency for this list is that Roto no longer provides his lists to SteamHistory, which means that a large portion of their markings no longer show up on the site.
